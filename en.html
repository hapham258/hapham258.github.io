Project Highlights

Direct SLAM based on Camera-LiDAR Fusion in Outdoor Environments

This video demonstrates the performance of a direct visual-laser SLAM in various outdoor environments. In the system, an improved image alignment algorithm, a fast cloud registration algorithm and a factor-graph-based windowed optimization algorithm are proposed. Combined with ikd-Tree data structure for map management, the system is proven to run smoothly in different challenging scenarios. This work is part of my master thesis at HCMUT, VNUHCM in 2022.


Simulation of LOS Path Following and SBG Obstacle Avoidance for USV

This video demonstrates how USV follows predefined paths of different parameterization, be it straight or bspline. The vehicle's guidance system is governed by LOS law that outputs reference heading for R-modelled sliding-mode heading controller to track. To avoid obstacle, SBG law is implemented, making decision based on measurements from a 2D LiDAR. This work is part of my collaboration with VIAM Lab at HCMUT, VNUHCM to realize autonomy in environmental monitoring for USV.


2D LiDAR SLAM in Outdoor Environments

This video demonstrates the performance of a 2D LiDAR SLAM in both synthetic and real environments. By taking advantage of robust weighting scheme, sliding-windowed optimization, fast scan-matcher and parallel computing, the system not only delivers stable performance in cluttered surroudings but also meets real-time constraint. This work is part of my bachelor thesis at HCMUT, VNUHCM in 2020.


Guidance and Control of Delivery AGV

This video demonstrates how a delivery AGV receives command from a monitor GUI and automatically drives itself towards the desired destination. To retrieve position and orientation, the vehicle relies on a RTK-GPS base receiver and an AHRS unit. Autonomous driving capability is then realized by LOS path following and PID heading control. This work is part of my project in 2019 FEEE Scientific Research for Student at HCMUT, VNUHCM.
